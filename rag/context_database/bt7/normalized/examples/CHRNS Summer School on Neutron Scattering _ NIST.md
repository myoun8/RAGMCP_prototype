# CHRNS Summer School on Neutron Scattering

The 27th Center for High Resolution Neutron Scattering (CHRNS) "School on Methods and Applications of Neutron Spectroscopy" was held virtually on January 28, 31 and February 2, 4, 7, 9, and 11, 2022. 

This school provides participants the opportunity to learn about a wide variety of neutron scattering techniques that utilize instruments at NIST, including:
* Disk Chopper Spectrometer (DCS)
* Backscattering Spectrometer (HFBS)
* BT-7 Double Focusing Triple-Axis Spectrometer
* Multi-Axis Crystal Spectrometer (MACS)
* Neutron Spin Echo Spectrometer (NSE)
* Neutron Diffraction

**Note:** Small angle neutron scattering (SANS) and neutron reflectometry (NR) techniques are not covered in this school.

## Course Overview
The course is targeted at individuals with little or no previous experience with neutron scattering methods. It combines introductory lectures and training in scattering techniques to familiarize participants with neutron scattering and its application to current research topics.

Attendance is limited to 40–56 students affiliated with North American universities, US national labs, and industry.

## Curriculum and Training
The course includes lectures and hands-on training in data reduction and analysis. Applicants select one pair of Neutron Scattering Experiments to study:

* **Triple Axis / MACS** (Hard Matter)
* **Neutron Spin Echo / High Flux Backscattering** (Soft Matter)
* **Disk Chopper / Diffraction** (Materials Chemistry)

## Sponsorship
The school is sponsored by the NCNR and by the National Science Foundation under the Center for High Resolution Neutron Scattering (CHRNS).
