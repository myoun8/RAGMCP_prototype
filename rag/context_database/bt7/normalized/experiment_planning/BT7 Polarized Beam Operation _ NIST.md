# BT7 Polarized Beam Operation

A polarized beam option has been developed for BT-7, utilizing $^3\text{He}$ polarizers immediately before and after the sample. This provides BT-7 with full polarized beam capability for experiments where a guide field (only) is applied at the sample position to control the direction of polarization and the resulting cross sections.

### Operational Details
*   **Polarization Control:** The system is fully operational. The $^3\text{He}$ itself can be inverted to achieve the alternate spin state before or after the sample.
*   **Guide Field:** An adjustable guide field at the sample position is under computer control to manipulate the polarization direction:
    *   Perpendicular to the scattering plane.
    *   Parallel to the scattering plane.
    *   Within an angular range in the scattering plane.
*   **Note:** Spin Flippers are no longer used.
*   **Alternative Option:** A vertical-field polarized beam option is available with the 7 T magnet at modest fields of 2 T and below.

### Scheduling Requirements
The use of the polarized beam option must be requested and scheduled well in advance of an experiment because:
1.  Optically pumping the cells takes approximately one day to achieve good $^3\text{He}$ polarization.
2.  Polarizers are shared across other instruments.

### Sample Mounting for Polarized Beam Operation
Special requirements must be followed when mounting samples for polarized beam measurements to prevent uncontrolled depolarization:

*   **Materials to Avoid:** No stainless steel of any kind should be used in or on the sample container.
*   **Hardware:** Use non-magnetic screws (e.g., aluminum or brass) to mount the sample onto the copper block (for example, in a closed cycle refrigerator).
*   **Low-Temperature Operation (< 1.5 K):** When using a dilution or $^3\text{He}$ refrigerator, avoid superconducting materials:
    *   **Cadmium (Cd):** Has caused problems ($T_C = 0.52\text{ K}$, $H_C = 2.8\text{ mT}$).
    *   **Pure Aluminum:** $T_C = 1.2\text{ K}$, $H_C = 10\text{ mT}$.
    *   **6061 Al Alloy:** Depending on composition and fabrication, it may have a lower or no transition but can still cause problems in some circumstances.
