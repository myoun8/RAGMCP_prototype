# Double Focusing Thermal Triple-Axis Spectrometers

> DEPRECATION NOTICE: This page is no longer being updated and the information may be out of date.

As part of the modernization of the thermal neutron spectrometers, a state-of-the-art triple-axis instrument is operational at the BT-7 thermal beam port. The instrument takes full advantage of the large 16 cm diameter beam tube, with 20×20 $\text{cm}^2$ double focusing monochromators. 

Two monochromators are available: a pyrolytic graphite (PG) monochromator and Cu(220). The analyzer system also provides horizontal focusing capability; together, these capabilities provide signals two orders-of-magnitude larger than those available with the original thermal triple-axis instruments. The analyzer system can also be used as a crystal array coupled with a position-sensitive detector in various configurations to allow the simultaneous collection of data over a range of $(Q, E)$.

## Instrument Dimensions

| Section | Distance |
| :--- | :--- |
| Source to Monochromator | 488 cm |
| Monochromator to Sample | 206 cm |
| Sample to Analyzer | Variable (165 cm to 229 cm) |
| Analyzer (center) to detector | 35 cm |

## Source and Beam Optics

### Beam Shutter
The experimental beam shutter is contained within the biological shield of the source with two positions: open and closed. 
- **Dimensions:** 6.4 cm wide at the exit, opening to 9 cm toward the source; 16 cm vertical opening.
- **Operation:** Opens or closes in approximately eleven seconds.

### Source Beam Limiters
Variable apertures are used to reduce the beam size from $6.4 \times 16 \text{ cm}^2$. Blades are composed of compressed lithium fluoride on the source-facing surface, with a 10 cm thick aluminum frame holder filled with $\text{B}_4\text{C}$.

### Filters
A pyrolytic graphite (PG) filter is available and can be translated in or out of the beam.

### Velocity Selector
Used to suppress higher-order wavelength neutrons. 
- **Operating Windows:** 5 meV, or from 11.5 meV to 60 meV.
- **Control:** Computer-controlled rotation to follow incident energy; can be translated in or out of the beam.

### Rotating Collimator Selector
The system includes three Söller-slit collimations: 10', 25', and 50', plus an 8.9 cm wide **Open** position for maximum intensity and horizontal energy focusing.

## Monochromator Drum

The drum is 213 cm in diameter with a 40.6 cm inner diameter. 
- **Angular Range:** From straight-through (optical alignment) to 115 degrees scattering angle.
- **Practical Range:** Approximately 16° to 75° on BT-7.

### Components
1.  **Stationary Pipe:** Extends from the drum edge (source side) to the center post for shielding. It contains a vertical magnetic field for polarized neutrons (compatible with white-beam polarizers like $^3\text{He}$). The beam onto the monochromator is 20 cm high $\times$ 11 cm wide.
2.  **Double-Focusing Monochromator Systems:** Currently available in PG graphite and Cu(220) (Ge(311) possible in the future). Each consists of 100 squares ($2 \times 2 \text{ cm}^2$), totaling $20 \times 20 \text{ cm}^2$. Systems are on an elevator for remote interchange.
3.  **Monochromatic Beam Optics:**
    - Guide field for polarized neutron beam operation.
    - Söller-slit collimations: 10', 25', 50', 80', and Open (beam width with collimation is 3.8 cm).
    - Horizontal beam focusing: Option for converging beam (horizontally and vertically) onto the sample (max sample size 2.5 cm wide $\times$ 5 cm high) using a tungsten shielding plug and mask.
    - Beam limiters: Two pairs (monochromator side and sample side) moving symmetrically.
    - Sample flux monitor.
    - $^3\text{He}$ polarizers available before and after the sample for polarized operation.
    - Space for additional options (e.g., extra PG filter or cold Be filter).

## Sample

The sample table system is fixed to the monochromator drum.
- **Travel:** 360 mm along the monochromatic beam direction (standard configuration is at the 200 mm position).
- **Motion:** Horizontal angular motion (rotation) and a second concentric horizontal motion for independent rotation of a horizontal field magnet.
- **Goniometer:** Includes x-y tilts and x-y translations. Mounted on an elevator with vertical travel of +2.5 cm above and 7.5 cm below the standard height of 15.25 cm. (The goniometer is removed to accommodate a 15 Tesla magnet).
- **Shielding:** Surrounded by a neutron-absorbing enclosure. A retractable beam stop is used to catch transmitted neutrons and gammas.

## Analyzer/Detector Systems

Analyzer systems attach to the $2\theta$ arm of the sample table via a hinge mechanism and a quick-coupling modular connection. Units move on air-pad suspensions.

### Current BT-7 System
The current system is a horizontally focused pyrolytic graphite analyzer.
- **Configuration:** 13 pyrolytic graphite elements (2 cm wide $\times$ 15 cm high).
- **Detection:** Individual detectors at the focus of the 13 blades, or a position-sensitive detector.
- **Monitoring:** 11 detectors integrated into the shielding behind the crystals monitor incoming flux. A separate diffraction detector is available for energy-integrated signals (Bragg peak intensity) and alignment.
- **Collimation:** 
    - **Internal (Computer Controlled):** 80' FWHM radial collimator; Söller collimators of 25', 50', 120', and Open.
    - **Sample-to-Analyzer:** Changed manually from outside the system.

### Future/Proposed Systems
- A system of 30 individual isolated analyzer/detector systems (similar to MACS cold triple-axis).
- Integration of a velocity selector into the analyzer system.
- Conventional double-focusing analyzer with a single shielded detector and interchangeable crystals.
