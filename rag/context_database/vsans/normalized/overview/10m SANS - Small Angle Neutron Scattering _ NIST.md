# 10m SANS - Small Angle Neutron Scattering

The 10 meter Small Angle Neutron Scattering (10m SANS) instrument is designed to measure correlations in spatial structure within solid and liquid materials. The technique measures deflections of a neutron beam as it passes through a sample of interest. The spectrum of deflected neutrons reveals important details such as:

*   **Material State:** Amorphous, crystalline, semi-crystalline, liquid crystalline, or combinations thereof.
*   **Composition Distribution:** Relative location of structures (e.g., water relative to a membrane pore, or a surfactant relative to a protein in a biopharmaceutical).
*   **Structural Evolution:** How structure changes with processing parameters critical to end applications.

The instrument provides ample space for sample environments, allowing the installation of equipment such as rheometers to measure the response of a fluid under shear while concurrently collecting SANS data. This allows structural observations to be connected to manufacturing processes including shear, high pressure, high temperature, and environmental exposure. SANS is a foundational technique for developing structure-property-performance relationships in solid materials, liquids, and mixtures.

## Operational Model
The 10m SANS is constructed similarly to the two 30 meter SANS instruments at the NCNR, with the primary difference being the overall maximum length (10 meters vs. 30 meters). 

The 10m SANS is operated by the **NIST nSoft consortium** ([www.nist.gov/nsoft](http://www.nist.gov/nsoft)), which partners NIST scientists with staff from member companies. Activities on the 10m SANS are performed for the benefit of the consortium, including:
*   Experiments performed with member companies.
*   Measurement development activities that provide new or enhanced capabilities for members.

Users wishing to access the 10m SANS are encouraged to contact the instrument scientists.

## Specifications/Capabilities

| Parameter | Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Q-Range** | $0.03\text{ nm}^{-1}$ to $6.0\text{ nm}^{-1}$ |
| **Size Regime** | $1\text{ nm}$ to $200\text{ nm}$ |
| **Source** | Neutron Guide (NG-B), cross-section: $60\text{ mm} \times 60\text{ mm}$ |
| **Monochromator** | Mechanical velocity selector with variable speed and pitch |
| **Wavelength Range** | $4.0\text{ \AA}$ to $20.0\text{ \AA}$ |
| **Wavelength Resolution** | $9\%$ to $30\% \Delta\lambda/\lambda$ (FWHM) |
| **Source-to-Sample Distance** | $3.0\text{ m}$ to $1\text{ m}$ via insertion of guide sections |
| **Collimation** | Circular pinhole collimation |
| **Sample Size** | $0\text{ mm}$ to $25\text{ mm}$ diameter |
| **Sample-to-Detector Distance** | $1.3\text{ m}$ to $5.2\text{ m}$ continuously variable |
| **Detector** | $640\text{ mm} \times 640\text{ mm}$ $^3\text{He}$ position-sensitive proportional counter with a $5.08\text{ mm} \times 5.08\text{ mm}$ resolution |
