# The very small angle neutron scattering instrument at the National Institute of Standards and Technology

A description and the performance of the very small angle neutron scattering (VSANS) diffractometer at the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) are presented. The instrument is designed to bridge the gap between traditional SANS and ultra-SANS (USANS) by providing a measurement range of momentum transfer $q$ from $2 \times 10^{-4}$ to $0.7\ \text{Å}^{-1}$.

## Instrument Overview

The VSANS instrument allows for the simultaneous measurement of the entire scattering angle range from $8 \times 10^{-5}$ to $\pi/6\ \text{rad}$ ($30^\circ$) using three separate detector carriages on rails holding nine 2D detector arrays.

## Key Technical Features

### 1. Momentum Transfer and Resolution
The $q$ range is determined by the relation $q \simeq 2\pi\theta/\lambda$. High $q$ resolution is achieved via multiple converging-beam collimation with circular pinholes, refractive lenses, and prisms. For experiments requiring higher beam intensity over relaxed vertical resolution, narrow slit collimation is used.

### 2. Wavelength Selection Options
The instrument provides four automated options for wavelength selection:
*   **Neutron Velocity Selector (NVS):** Provides a triangular distribution with $\Delta\lambda/\lambda = 12.5\%$.
*   **Supermirror Deflection Filter:** A cutoff filter reflecting $\lambda > 8.3\ \text{Å}$, combined with a Be filter cutting $\lambda < 4\ \text{Å}$, resulting in $\Delta\lambda/\lambda \simeq 44\%$.
*   **HOPG Double Monochromator:** Two reflections of Highly Oriented Pyrolytic Graphite crystals provide high resolution ($\Delta\lambda/\lambda = 0.8\text{--}1.0\%$).
*   **Filter Only:** Utilizes the full transmission of the Be–Bi beam filter.

### 3. Detector Configuration
To cover a wide angular range without time offsets, the instrument uses three carriages:
*   **Rear Carriage:** High-resolution 2D detector.
*   **Front and Middle Carriages:** Four 2D tube detectors that can be arranged into a "picture frame" of adjustable size, allowing the inner unscattered beam to pass to downstream detectors.

### 4. Polarization Analysis
Full polarization analysis is available using:
*   A high-performance supermirror polarizer.
*   An RF spin flipper (flipping ratios $> 100$).
*   A high-efficiency $^3\text{He}$ polarization analyzer.

## Source and Beam Optics
The instrument is located on the NG-3 guide ($60 \times 150\ \text{mm}$, $m=1.18$). To minimize background, a Be–Bi filter (cooled to $77\ \text{K}$) is used to attenuate core gamma rays and fast neutrons. The entire instrument is rotated by $0.3^\circ$ to ensure the farthest detector is not in the direct line of sight of the source.
